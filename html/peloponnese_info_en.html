THE PELOPONNESE SOUTHERN GREECE

Peloponnese - also known as the Peloponese, Pelopponese, Peloponnesus & Peloponnesos - a peninsula separated from mainland Greece by the Corinth Canal

The Peloponnese peninsula has for many year been considered an island due to it being separated by the Corinth Canal and is made up of 6 prefectures, namely Corinthia, Argolida, Arcadia, Elia, Achaia, Lakonia and Messinia.

The countryside is diverse with a high mountain range over 2000m running down the middle of the peninsula, long sandy beaches and ragged coastlines with many coves and small natural harbours. The Peloponnese has something to offer to everyone, is perfect for beach holidays with a touch of culture.

Visit the Arcadian mountain villages where the chiefs of the resistance during the Turkish occupation hid out, the Corinth Canal, Monemvasia (Greece's Rock of Gibraltar), the stone castle-like houses of Mani, the Cave of Lakes and the famous Diros Caves. Ski during the Winter months on the mountains of Mainalo and at Kalavrita and visit Mts Parnona and Taygetos at any time of the year and walk among the amazing flora and fauna. These mountains have their own micro-climates and are full of rare flowers and wildlife.

If you are interested in history then visit the sites of Ancient Olympia, home of the Olympic Games, Ancient Nemea (the Nemean Games), Ancient Corinth, Acrocorinth, Byzantine Mystra, Sparta and many more.

With a great combination of history, beaches, activities, breathtaking scenery and splendid nature the Peloponnese is a superb choice for your holidays in Greece.
